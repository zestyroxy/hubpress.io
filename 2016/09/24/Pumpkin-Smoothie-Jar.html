Pumpkin Smoothie Jar

2016-09-24 00:00:00 [vegan, pumpkin, smoothie, fall, breakfast] Roxanne Joncas

Since fall (my favourite season) is here I decided to bake three different pumpkin recipies! Here is the first one: Pumpkin Smoothie Jar. #PumpkinAllTheThings

This is an easy and delicious breakfast. It smells so good, it’ll make you want to curl up with a good book and a hot cup of coffee by the fire while you slowly enjoy every bite.

Pumpkin Smoothie

The first step to making a pumpkin smoothie jar is to make the smoothie. For this recipe, I used the pumpkin purée that I make myself last night, but you can definitely use store bought purée. Scroll down to the end of this post to read how to make your very own pumpkin purée.

Smoothie Ingredients

  • 2 medjool dates

  • 130 g pumpkin purée

  • 160 ml almond milk (unsweetened)

  • 0.5 g nutmeg

  • 0.5 g all spice

  • 1.5 g cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Put all the ingredients in a blender and mix until the consitency is smoothe.

Smoothie Only Option: You can also just drink the smoothie on it’s own. I would suggest simply adding a bit more almond milk to thin it out a little.

Pumpkin Smoothie Jar

Ingredients

  • pumpkin smoothie

  • 30 g chia seeds

  • 70 g vegan granola of your choice

  • 80 g plain, vanilla, or coconut yogurt

  • 20 g pumpkin seeds

Instructions

  1. Arrange in a bowl or jar in an aesthetically pleasing way or if you’re too hungry just throw it all in there :)

  2. Add fruits or other nuts according to your cravings today!

You’re done! Now grab a good book and enjoy!

Pumpkin Purée

I prefer to make my own pumpkin purée when pumpkin are in season. It just tastes so good and I know the only ingredient is, well pumpkin.

Go to the store and get yourself a pumpkin. Pro tip: make it the night before you want to make your favourite pumpkin recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 small pumpkin - will make about 600 g of purée

Instructions

  1. Set the oven to 190 C.

  2. Cut the pumpkin in half with a sharp knife and empty our the guts. Keep the seeds and rince them if you want to roast them later.

  3. Cut the pumpkin halves lengthwise into six slices.

  4. Arrange the pumpkin slices outter skin down on a baking pan, no oil is needed.

  5. Bake the pumpkin for one hour, or until you can easily poke a fork through the skin.

  6. Once ready, take out of the even and let it cool.

  7. After it has cooled down, use you hands to peel the outer skin off the pulp.

  8. Put the pulp into a food processor and purée (or mash with a for or potato masher).

Voilà! Keep your purée in the fridge for up to a week or freeze some for later use.
